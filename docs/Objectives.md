# Project Objectives

## Primary Objective

To develop a student-centric web platform that assists students in finding verified accommodations and compatible roommates by providing personalized recommendations and decision-support features.

---

## Specific Objectives

### 1. Accommodation Discovery

To provide a centralized platform where students can search and explore verified PGs and rental accommodations based on their preferences and requirements.

### 2. Roommate Compatibility Analysis

To design and implement a Living Compatibility Index (LCI) that matches students with compatible roommates based on lifestyle preferences and living habits.

### 3. Personalized Recommendations

To recommend suitable accommodations and potential roommates using user preferences, compatibility scores, and filtering criteria.

### 4. Decision Support

To help students make informed housing decisions through reviews, ratings, and accommodation comparison features.

### 5. Communication Platform

To facilitate communication between students, property owners, and potential roommates through an integrated real-time chat system.

### 6. Property Management

To enable property owners to create, manage, and update accommodation listings efficiently.

### 7. Platform Administration

To provide administrators with tools to verify listings, manage users, and maintain the quality and integrity of the platform.

### 8. User Experience and Security

To develop a secure, responsive, and user-friendly web application that ensures data privacy and provides a seamless experience across devices.

---

## Expected Outcomes

Upon successful completion of the project, the system should:

- Reduce the time and effort required to find suitable accommodation.
- Improve roommate compatibility and reduce living conflicts.
- Increase trust through verified listings and reviews.
- Provide a centralized platform specifically designed for student housing needs.
- Demonstrate the practical implementation of recommendation systems and compatibility-based matching in a real-world application.
